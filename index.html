Principal Investigator

Prof. Vikas Gautam

Prof. Vikas Gautam (MBBS, MD, PhD, MAMS, FRCP Edin) is Professor in the Department of Medical Microbiology at PGIMER, Chandigarh — an Institute of National Importance ranked 2nd among medical colleges in India. As a Clinical Microbiologist, he actively bridges bench research and bedside care, managing both laboratory diagnostic services and infectious disease consultancy in ICUs and high-dependency wards.

His research spans antimicrobial resistance (AMR) mechanisms, non-fermenting Gram-negative bacteria (especially Burkholderia and Stenotrophomonas), vaccine-preventable diseases (VPD), and wound care innovation. Notably, he described the novel species Stenotrophomonas sepilia (2021) and leads the WHO-designated VPD Surveillance Centre covering six Northern States of India.

His wound-care innovation — a herbal antiseptic formulation — won the First Prize at BIRAC BRIC 2020 (IKP, IMTECH-CSIR) and is currently undergoing Randomised Controlled Trials at PGIMER and AIIMS New Delhi, with clinicians calling it a "game-changer" in wound care.

What We Study

Research Focus Areas

Our work spans fundamental and translational microbiology, driven by clinical relevance and a commitment to improving patient outcomes.

Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR)

Mechanisms of resistance in Burkholderia cepacia complex, Stenotrophomonas, Acinetobacter baumannii, and Klebsiella pneumoniae; efflux pumps, carbapenemases, and novel resistance genes.

Stenotrophomonas & Burkholderia Biology

Taxonomy, virulence characterisation, genomics, and pathogenic pathways of these non-fermenting Gram-negative bacteria, including the description of the novel species S. sepilia.

Vaccine-Preventable Diseases (VPD)

WHO-designated surveillance of Diphtheria and Pertussis across six Northern Indian states; molecular epidemiology, genomics, and natural compound formulations against C. diphtheriae.

Bacterial Genomics & Pathogenomics

Whole-genome sequencing, MLST, pan-genome analyses, and molecular epidemiology to understand clonal expansion and virulence evolution in clinical isolates.

Wound Care Innovation & Natural Compounds

Development and clinical evaluation of herbal antiseptic formulations for wound care, with ongoing RCTs at PGIMER and AIIMS New Delhi; prosthetic joint and burn infections.

Clinical & Translational Microbiology

Pharmacokinetics-pharmacodynamics (PK-PD) of antimicrobials, antibiotic stewardship, nosocomial infections, sepsis epidemiology, and infection control guidelines.
